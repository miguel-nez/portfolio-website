Simplex
Webapp for Supporting Immigrants in Germany

UX Research & Design, UI Design, Information Architecture, Interaction Design

Figjam, Figma, Miro, Photoshop

6 Months

Challange

Newcomers in Germany, especially those without a high level in the official language, are subject to adaptative and integrational difficulties. Bureaucracy, legal issues or day-to-day obstacles become even greater fears for those who don’t have the expertise after years in the country.

Solution

Simplex offers assistance to immigrants on three different levels: it allows the user to get in touch with experts such as lawyers or translators; a help center containing guides for particular experiences and explanations to bureaucratic procedures; and a moderated community for sharing commong problems and common solutions.

Why Simplex

Since I moved to Germany five years ago I’ve been in touch with immigrants from very different backgrounds. The wide majority felt a lack of security regarding legal issues, especially those who did not have that many German-born connections. When I was given the task of designing an Expert App I thought of it as an opportunity to be of help to this community.


I learnt a bunch of things throughout the course of this project, so the original concept was adapted accordingly. One of such mutations took place after conducting the first interviews, where I discovered that legal matters were just one of the main concerns in our user’s everyday life and, therefore, I studied other possible fields wherein the app could offer assistance.

Initial Problem Statement

Living in Germany, it is often discussed how inaccessible the legal system can be, even for locals. At the same time, coming here from a foreign country to live and work, or study, is already a complex experience: add some incident at the workplace, with a roommate or the landperson and it can get pretty hectic.



Driven by the principle that every person should be equal before the law, it is fundamental to aknowledge that not everyone has the same tools to interact with the system. That’s how Simplex was born.

Our users need a way to find solutions to their legal issues because they face disadvantages that make these processes frustrating and difficult. We will know this to be true when we see that they can solve their legal problems after using our app.

Competitor Analysis

I started off the discovery phase by taking a look at some products out there in the market. I was focused back then in creating a legal assitance service, and found a few app –very few– that also did. I chose two for a deeper analysis: Advocado, a German one, and LexMeet, a foreign one. While the first one was mainly directed to be a meeting point for german lawyers and clients, the second one offered a wider range of features and targeted Filipinxs living abroad and international businesses.



Observations

  • There’s not much of a competence in Germany
  • Offering a free initial consultation is pretty much the norm
  • Manuals are a good aditional feature
  • Not friendly for immigrants
  • Target user is young, 25-45.

Features

  • Free first consultation
  • Community feature
  • Manuals & tips
  • Full service over app
  • Language Preference

User Survey & Interviews

I conducted three interviews and a brief online survey in order to know my target users better, their worries and their needs as well as some demographics for my future persona(s). Since I was focused on creating a legal assistance app at the start of this project I also centered my questions on the user’s experience with the German legal system and its bureaucracy issues.

Insights Summary

  • Many issues stay unresolved because of fear or lack of a helping hand
  • Mistrust when checking unknown blogs and forums online
  • People long for a place where they can easily check day-to-day issues, rather than only legal stuff
  • Tendecy to avoid contacting a lawyer due to the price, language difficulties or time-constraints.

User Persona

After the initial research I wanted to encapsulate the characteristics of our target audience in a way that would bring me closer to their perspective. I organized the data I collected on the survey and the interviews and crafted a persona.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Hand-drawn sketches were the first step toward transforming the research findings into a prototype, defining the features and structure of the app.

Usability Testing

With a simple prototype created on Figma, I put the features, navigation and general purpose of the app down to test: 5 remote and in-person interviews.


The app’s overall concept had worked well, the users were satisfied with the outcome of their interactions with it and the main issues were caused by a structure which didn’t feel natural and an interface that was a bit chaotic. Therefore I reorganized the information and the way the user would access it, cutting down on “clicks” and making it visually more simple but coherent.

Check the test results table here

Final Protoype

My goal was to create an app that brought together three common ways to getting assistance, so I designed a simple menu where the user could easily access each of them and then explore the different options within them.


I wanted to apply an eye-catching palette that would bring patience but also energy to a probably distressed user.
